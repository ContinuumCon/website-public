Community Behavior & Conduct Policy

Welcome! We’re excited to have you here.

This conference is a place for learning, sharing ideas, and supporting one another. We’re a big community of learners—newcomers, professionals, speakers, sponsors, and volunteers—and we want everyone to feel safe, respected, and included.

Be kind. Be constructive. Help others level up.

Our Standard: Respect + Good Faith

We expect participants to:

  • Treat others with respect, even when you disagree
  • Ask questions with curiosity, not hostility
  • Give feedback constructively and professionally
  • Assume good intent and communicate clearly
  • Help create a welcoming environment for everyone

If you’re unsure whether something is appropriate, choose the safer, kinder option.

This Applies Everywhere

This policy applies to all conference spaces, including:

  • Live streams (Twitch / YouTube / X/Twitter / other platforms)
  • Discord channels, DMs, voice, and screen share
  • Chat, Q&A, workshops, and networking sessions
  • Speaker, sponsor, and attendee interactions
  • Any conference-related communication or content

Zero Tolerance Behavior

We have zero tolerance for abuse or harassment of any kind.

That includes, but is not limited to:

  • Hate speech, slurs, or discriminatory comments
  • Harassment, intimidation, stalking, or bullying
  • Sexual harassment or unwanted sexual attention
  • Threats of violence or encouraging harm
  • Doxxing or sharing someone’s private info (even “as a joke”)
  • Targeted insults, personal attacks, or humiliation
  • Trolling, disruptive behavior, or intentional derailment
  • Spam, scams, impersonation, or malicious links/files
  • Encouraging self-harm or glorifying harm to others

If your behavior makes someone feel unsafe or unwelcome, we will treat it seriously.

Keep It Safe, Legal, and Ethical

We’re here to learn. That means:

  • Don’t share or request illegal content (stolen data, credentials, etc.)
  • Don’t post real personal data, private logs, or sensitive screenshots
  • Don’t attempt to exploit other attendees, speakers, or conference systems
  • Don’t use this community to recruit for harm, fraud, or harassment campaigns

Security topics are welcome—harmful behavior is not.

Community Values: Support Over Shame

We actively encourage a culture of:

  • Helping others learn without gatekeeping
  • Offering support and resources when people struggle
  • Keeping discussions professional and solution-focused
  • Being patient with beginners and respectful of different experience levels

No one should be mocked for asking a sincere question.

Moderation and Enforcement

To protect the community, the conference team and moderators may take action at any time, including:

  • Removing messages or content
  • Issuing warnings
  • Muting or restricting chat access
  • Removing someone from Discord or live sessions
  • Revoking a ticket and access to conference content/services

Refund Policy for Enforcement

If someone violates this policy, we reserve the right to remove them without warning and without refund, depending on the situation.

We’re not interested in “drama management.” We’re here to protect the experience for everyone else.

Reporting Issues

If something feels wrong, please report it. You’re not “being a problem”—you’re helping us keep the space safe.

You can report issues by:

  • Messaging a moderator in Discord
  • Using the event’s official contact method (email/support form)
  • Flagging content on the streaming platform when appropriate

When reporting, include:

  • What happened
  • Where it happened (Discord channel, stream chat, etc.)
  • Any screenshots or links (if available)

Our Promise

We will do our best to:

  • Respond quickly and respectfully
  • Take reports seriously
  • Protect privacy where we can
  • Act in the best interest of community safety

This is a learning environment. Respect is non-negotiable.

Thank you for being part of the community — and for helping us make this a space people want to return to year after year.
